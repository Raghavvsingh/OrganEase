# Donor Document Requirements

## Required Documents for Donor Registration

All living organ donors must upload the following 4 documents during registration:

### 1. Government ID (Aadhaar / Passport) ‚úÖ
**Purpose:** Identity verification to prevent brokers and impersonation

**Requirements:**
- Clear photo of Aadhaar card (both sides) OR Passport
- All details must be legible
- Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 5MB

**Why needed:** Ensures authentic identity and prevents fraudulent donor registrations.

---

### 2. Medical Fitness Certificate üè•
**Purpose:** Proof that donor is medically fit for organ donation

**Requirements:**
- Must be issued by a registered hospital or qualified doctor
- Should clearly state:
  - Donor is medically fit
  - Organ(s) intended for donation are healthy
- Must be recent (within last 30 days)
- Should include doctor's signature and hospital stamp
- Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 5MB

**Why needed:** Ensures donor's safety and organ viability for transplantation.

---

### 3. Blood Group & Compatibility Reports ü©∏
**Purpose:** Blood group proof and basic organ health verification

**Requirements:**
- Official blood group test report from certified lab
- Basic organ function tests (if available):
  - Kidney donation: Creatinine levels, GFR
  - Liver donation: LFT reports
  - General: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 5MB

**Why needed:** Essential for matching with compatible recipients and ensuring safe donation.

---

### 4. Consent Declaration (Signed) üìùüî¥
**Purpose:** Legal proof of voluntary donation

**Requirements:**
- Download OrganEase consent form template
- Print the form
- Fill in all personal details
- Read and understand all declaration points
- Sign and date the form
- Optional: Get witness signatures
- Scan or photograph the signed form
- Upload the signed copy
- Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 5MB

**Why needed:** 
- Legal compliance with Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994
- Confirms "I am donating voluntarily"
- Protects donor from coercion
- Required for hospital authorization

## How to Download and Complete the Consent Form

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Access the Form:**
   - Go to donor registration page
   - Click "View/Download Consent Form (HTML)"
   - Form will open in a new browser tab

2. **Save as PDF:**
   - Press `Ctrl+P` (Windows) or `Cmd+P` (Mac)
   - Select "Save as PDF" as destination
   - Click Save

3. **Print the Form:**
   - Print the saved PDF on A4 paper
   - Use good quality printer for clear text

4. **Fill Out the Form:**
   - Use BLACK or BLUE pen only
   - Fill Section A: Your personal details
   - Check Section B: Select organs you consent to donate
   - Read Section C: All 10 declaration points carefully

5. **Sign the Form:**
   - Sign in the "Donor's Signature" box
   - Add today's date and place
   - Optional: Add thumb impression
   - Optional: Get 2 witnesses to sign Section D

6. **Scan/Photograph:**
   - Scan the completed form in color
   - OR take a clear photo with good lighting
   - Ensure all text is readable
   - Save as PDF or JPG

7. **Upload:**
   - Return to donor registration page
   - Upload the signed consent form
   - Wait for confirmation message

## Verification Process

### What Happens After Upload:

1. **Submission:** All 4 documents uploaded successfully
2. **Review:** Hospital authorities review within 2-3 business days
3. **Verification:** Documents checked for authenticity and completeness
4. **Notification:** Email and dashboard notification about status
5. **Activation:** Once verified, profile becomes visible to compatible recipients

## Important Legal Information

### Transplantation of Human Organs and Tissues Act, 1994

This consent form and document requirements comply with Indian law regarding organ donation:

- **Voluntary Donation:** No coercion or financial compensation allowed
- **Age Requirement:** Donor must be 18-65 years old
- **Medical Fitness:** Certified by qualified medical professional
- **Informed Consent:** Full understanding of risks and procedures
- **Revocable:** Consent can be withdrawn anytime before surgery
- **Hospital Authorization:** Final approval by transplant committee

### Your Rights as a Donor:

‚úÖ Right to full information about the procedure
‚úÖ Right to withdraw consent at any time
‚úÖ Right to confidentiality of medical information
‚úÖ Right to refuse donation without penalty
‚úÖ Right to medical care if complications arise

## FAQ - Document Upload

**Q: Can I upload documents later?**
A: No, all 4 documents are mandatory during registration. Profile will not be created without them.

**Q: What if I don't have a medical fitness certificate?**
A: Visit any registered hospital or doctor, explain you're registering as an organ donor, and request a fitness certificate.

**Q: Can I use a digital signature on the consent form?**
A: No, physical signature is required for legal validity. Print, sign, and scan/photograph.

**Q: How long are these documents valid?**
A: Medical certificates should be recent (within 30 days). Other documents remain valid as long as information is current.

**Q: What if my document upload fails?**
A: Ensure file is under 5MB and in PDF/JPG/PNG format. Try compressing large images. Contact support if issue persists.

**Q: Can I update documents after registration?**
A: Yes, you can update documents from your donor dashboard if verification is rejected or documents expire.
